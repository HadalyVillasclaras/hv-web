    BIO

    Hadaly Villasclaras is a visual artist and software developer whose work focuses on linking the processes of traditional artistic practice with the media and narratives of the digital era. In doing so, she explores themes related to visual culture and the internet, as well as the systems of representation and fictional practices that are manifested around them.


    She holds a BFA and a MFA from University of Málaga and a Higher Technical Degree in Software/Web Development from Ilerna School. Her work has been included in exhibitions and lectures such as “Nuevas Identidades Digitales” at Loom Festival (Barcelona, 2021), “Yami Ichi. Tianguis de Intercambios Digitales” at Centro de Cultura Digital (Ciudad de México, 2020), ATENEA – Women in Artistic Technologies at Universitat Politècnica de Valencia (2018), “Neighbours IV” at CAC (Málaga, 2017), EDÉN (Casa Antillón, Madrid, 2020), “Aprender a dejar de pintar” at Espai Subsol (L’Hospitalet de Llobregat (Barcelona, 2020)), “A Secas. Artistas Andaluces de Ahora” at CAAC (Sevilla, 2015), “BLUR” at Phoenix Institute of Contemporary Art (phICA) & MCC Art Gallery, EEUU (2018) and “Everything must be rendered perfectly” (solo_show, 2020).
