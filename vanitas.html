    A flitting thing
    (Vanitas)

    A Flitting Thing (Vanitas) is a revisiting of the Vanitas through the iconographic and symbolic appropriation of these popular pictorial genre representations from the 16th and 17th centuries.


    These symbols have been isolated and painted on small canvases, and displaced into a kind of Mise-en-scène environment where painting, symbolism and iconography are merged with objects from the realm of ornamentation and clothing.


    The motifs of ephemerality, futility and emptiness of life commonly included in vanitas symbolic works are reviewed from a deliberately ambiguous setting in which one does not know what is the "transcendent" art object or the "transient" decorative item.
